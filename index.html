◆ EBITDA Margins Stable

Global telecom EBITDA margins hold at ~35%. Capital intensity moderating from 17-18% (2022-23) to ~15.9% (2024), signaling a shift from build-phase to monetization.

⚠ Pricing Pressure Persists

ARPU remains largely stagnant across mature markets. Only a minority of operators report meaningful growth, underscoring fragile mobile monetization.

Competitive boundaries between carriers, cable, hyperscalers, and satellite firms continue blurring as companies race to bundle wireline, wireless, and edge-cloud.

52% of small cells already in enterprise. CBRS-based private networks in factories, hospitals, ports. T-Mobile public safety slices: $200M/yr.

5
Fixed Wireless Access (FWA)CAGR: 15-20%Now-2027

14M+ households connected. Legitimate broadband alternative in areas without fiber. Drives ongoing tower upgrades and new site activity.

◆ Estimating & Preconstruction

As build complexity grows (BEAD compliance, BABA, multi-tech deployments), demand for skilled estimators who understand telecom construction is rising. Preconstruction roles bridge field knowledge and project economics.

⚠ Data Center / AI Adjacency

Telcos finding indirect AI revenue: selling fiber to data centers, leasing tower sites for edge compute, building private networks for AI facilities. New market entry points.

➤ Contractor Consolidation

NATE/carrier agreements reshaping contracting. Turf model elimination and 1099 crew restrictions favor larger, certified firms. Creates acquisition opportunities and competitive moats.
